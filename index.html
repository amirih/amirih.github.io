About

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science and Informatics at Emory University advised by Prof. Andreas Züfle. My research focuses on high performance software systems for large scale spatial data, human mobility modeling, and data driven public health applications.

I design and build scalable, hardware aware software, simulation frameworks, and datasets that remove computational bottlenecks from modern scientific workflows. My work combines systems, parallel processing, spatial computing, and epidemiology to enable larger experiments, faster iteration, and more reliable insights.

Research Interests

  • High performance and parallel computing
  • Software engineering for scientific computing
  • Spatial data systems and trajectory analytics
  • Location based services and mobility modeling
  • Data driven public health and epidemiological modeling

Research

My research aims to make large scale data processing and simulation practical for researchers working with mobility, spatial, and epidemiological data. I focus on efficient system design, parallelism, and careful software engineering so that complex scientific workflows can be executed reliably at scale.

Current Themes

  • High performance software systems for human mobility data generation and analysis
  • Scalable simulation frameworks for epidemiology and public health decision support
  • Global point of interest and trajectory datasets for urban and spatial analytics
  • Compiler and SIMD based optimization for performance critical kernels
  • LLM assisted methods for spatial trajectory pattern mining and anomaly detection

Teaching

My teaching philosophy centers on accessibility, structured learning, and active engagement. I design courses that balance conceptual foundations with hands on systems work, using a mix of lectures, labs, projects, and gamified elements to support different learning styles.

I emphasize clear structure, multiple assessment pathways, and modern tooling such as autograders and learning platforms so that students can focus on understanding systems concepts and building confidence in their ability to solve open ended problems.
