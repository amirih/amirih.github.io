About

I build high performance software systems, data pipelines, and simulations for spatial data and human mobility. I aim to make large scale epidemiological and urban computing research faster, more reproducible, and more realistic.

Research Highlights

  • Patterns of Life simulation frameworks enabling city and national scale mobility and disease spread modeling with realistic temporal and behavioral dynamics.
  • Global trajectory and point of interest datasets supporting spatial analysis, mobility pattern mining, and urban decision making.
  • Performance optimized spatial data processing and reproducible scientific workflows for public health research.

Teaching Focus

I teach systems and computing foundations with an emphasis on clarity, hands-on learning, and student success. I have helped deliver multiple courses at Emory including:

  • Introduction to Computer Science
  • Data Structures and Algorithms
  • Database Systems
  • Spatial Computing
