Research snapshot

One line version: I design high performance software and data for realistic human mobility and spatial simulations that support public health and urban decision making.

Questions I care about

  • How do we simulate realistic human mobility at national scale fast enough to be useful in practice.
  • How can spatial data systems and trajectories support public health decisions instead of being a bottleneck.
  • How do we build research software that is fast, reproducible, and usable by non systems experts.

What I build

  • Patterns of life based simulators for human mobility and disease spread.
  • Large scale trajectory and point of interest datasets for spatial analysis.
  • High performance pipelines that turn raw data into ready to use research inputs.

Where I fit in a department

  • Core systems and high performance computing.
  • Databases and spatial data management.
  • Data driven public health, computational epidemiology, and urban computing.

Selected papers

For a full and current list, please see my Google Scholar. Below are a few representative pieces of work.
